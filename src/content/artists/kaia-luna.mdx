## Who They Are

Kaia Luna makes electronic art pop that sounds like late-night drives through fog — layered synths, whispered vocals, and beats that hit somewhere between Björk and FKA twigs. Based in Portland, she produces, writes, and engineers everything herself from a converted garage studio she built with plywood and moving blankets.

## Before The Signal

Kaia grew up in a small town outside Portland where the closest thing to a music scene was a church choir and a guy who played acoustic covers at the farmers market. She didn't touch a DAW until she was 19, learning GarageBand on a MacBook she borrowed from a roommate. Her first year of production was brutal — hundreds of hours making tracks she describes as "unlistenable." She worked at a plant nursery during the day and produced from midnight to 4 AM. The turning point came when she posted a rough demo called "Dissolve" on SoundCloud. A Spotify playlist curator found it, reached out, and put it on Pollen. Within two months she went from 800 monthly listeners to 200K. She still works at the nursery two days a week.
